# PM-Tools: UK Interior Design Project Management Software

## Origin
Sara Mansoor (interior designer, UK) reached out 2026-02-20 about a gap in PM software for UK design studios. She's part of multiple designer groups and hears this complaint consistently. She mentioned Houzz Pro, EstiPC, and Programa as reference points but feels none fully serve UK designers.

## Sara's Vision (from WhatsApp 2026-02-20)
An AI-powered blend of Houzz Pro + EstiPC + Programa that handles:
- Order tracking (placed, delivered)
- Construction schedule management
- Designer purchase tracking with markup calculations
- Dual invoicing (client-facing vs trade invoices for designers)
- Preloaded supplier details for easy ordering
- Auto-generated invoices/documentation with client sharing
- Mood board/visual creation
- Basic plan drawings
- Multi-seat subscription for studio teams

She sees this as a partnership opportunity — she brings domain expertise, client network, and knows where to promote. She wants fair compensation, not a favour.

## Competitor Analysis

### 1. EstiPC / EstiMac (UK-built, ~20 years old)
- **What:** Desktop app for interior design project admin. Taught at KLC School of Design.
- **Best at:** Markup tracking, dual invoicing (client vs supplier docs), UK-specific
- **Price:** GBP 250-350/user/year
- **Fatal flaws:** Desktop-only (no cloud/portal), no visual tools, no mobile, dated UI
- **Verdict:** Best financial engine for UK designers, but stuck in 2005

### 2. Houzz Pro (Global, US-based)
- **What:** All-in-one platform, 3M+ pros globally. Available in UK.
- **Best at:** Lead generation, 3D floor plans, client portal, CRM
- **Price:** From GBP 54/month; full plans GBP 3,500-4,200+/year
- **Fatal flaws:** 3D planner "very limited", invoicing "extremely slow", aggressive sales, weak procurement/markup, expensive
- **Verdict:** Marketing platform disguised as PM tool. Weak on actual design studio operations

### 3. Programa (Australian-founded)
- **What:** SaaS built by/for interior designers. 5,000+ users. 4.8 stars Capterra.
- **Best at:** FF&E specs, product library with web clipper, client approvals, procurement
- **Price:** ~GBP 485/user/year (up to 3 users)
- **Fatal flaws:** Invoicing still in Beta and "clunky", mood boards "limiting", no floor plans, no FreeAgent integration, no preloaded suppliers
- **Verdict:** Closest competitor. Improving fast but still has gaps

### 4. Studio Designer (US, 30+ years)
- **Price:** GBP 57-87/user/month
- **Best at:** Procurement workflow, vendor tracking, margin analysis
- **Weakness:** US-centric, no UK tax/accounting, no visual tools

### 5. DesignFiles (US)
- **Price:** GBP 39-55/month + GBP 20/extra user
- **Best at:** Visual design — mood boards, floor plans, 3D renders
- **Weakness:** Basic procurement, no dual invoicing, no construction scheduling

### 6. Indema (Newer entrant)
- **Price:** GBP 56-60/user/month
- **Best at:** True all-in-one attempt — PM, accounting, comms, purchasing, Gantt charts
- **Weakness:** Expensive onboarding (USD 950+), small user base, limited UK reviews

### 7. Others
- **Mydoma Studio:** Strong client portal, weak procurement (GBP 51/user/month)
- **Fohlio:** Enterprise FF&E spec, overkill for small studios (GBP 96+/month)
- **DesignerLogic:** Claims 8,000+ preloaded suppliers (mostly US), Xero export

## Genuine Gaps (What Nobody Does)

### 1. No single tool does everything well
The market is fragmented. Designers use EstiPC for financials + Programa for specs + DesignFiles for visuals + Monday.com for scheduling. Nobody integrates all four pillars.

### 2. Construction scheduling is absent
No interior design PM tool offers proper construction scheduling with contractor coordination. Designers bolt on generic tools (Monday.com, Trello, Outbuild).

### 3. Preloaded UK supplier database = zero
No tool comes preloaded with UK trade suppliers (fabric houses, furniture makers, lighting, tiles). This is the single biggest differentiator opportunity.

### 4. Dual invoicing is rare
Only EstiPC properly separates client-facing docs (with markup) from supplier-facing docs (at trade cost). Most tools treat invoicing as one stream.

### 5. UK accounting integration is an afterthought
FreeAgent (most popular UK small business accounting) is supported by ZERO interior design platforms. VAT handling and GBP are secondary to USD everywhere.

## Verdict: Is This Worth Building?

### YES — but with caveats

**The gap is real.** Sara has correctly identified a fragmented market with no single dominant solution for UK interior designers. The opportunity is in **integration + UK-specificity**, not any single missing feature.

**The strongest wedge:**
> UK-first platform with preloaded UK supplier database + proper dual invoicing/VAT + construction scheduling + client portal — then add visual tools over time.

**Revenue potential:**
- ~6,000 UK businesses at GBP 300-500/user/year = GBP 5-15M/year ceiling UK-only
- Expandable to EU (similar fragmentation) for 3-5x multiplier
- Not venture-scale without going global, but very viable as a profitable SaaS business

**What Sara may not know:**
- Programa is improving fast and could close many gaps in 12-18 months
- EstiPC + Programa together already cover ~80% of her requirements (in two tools)
- Building something that beats Programa on collaboration + EstiPC on financials + Houzz Pro on visuals is a massive engineering effort

**Recommended approach if pursuing:**
1. Start with the unsexy but defensible wedge: **UK supplier database + dual invoicing + construction scheduling**
2. Integrate with Xero/FreeAgent from day one
3. Add client portal early (table stakes)
4. Partner with UK trade suppliers for preloaded catalogs (moat)
5. Add mood board / visual tools later (or integrate with Canva/Figma)
6. AI angle: auto-generate specs from mood boards, smart supplier matching, predictive scheduling
